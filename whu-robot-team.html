WHU United RoboCup 2026 Humanoid Robot League (Adult Division) Self-improvement, Perseverance, Seeking Truth, and Pioneering Innovation

With the rapid development and wide application of the Unitree G1 platform, the WHU United team is developing a set of RoboCup 2026 humanoid robot adult division systems, which combines low-level motion control based on reinforcement learning with software stacks suitable for competitions. We attempt to implement advanced reinforcement learning-based motion primitives on the G1, including stable walking, autonomous standing recovery and kicking motions. In addition, we draw on the modular RoboCup architectures of Booster-T1 and B-Human, port and adapt them to the G1 platform, and provide core components required for adult division competitions, such as perception-action integration, motion interfaces and behavior execution. To improve team-level performance, we further integrate multi-agent strategy modules from previous 3D simulation experience, including passing strategies and dynamic role switching of forwards based on game scenarios. We hope these implementations can lay a solid foundation for reliable on-field operation and provide support for the subsequent development of RoboCup 2026.

Core Robot Specifications

Unitree G1 Comp is a competition-level humanoid robot launched by Unitree Robotics, specially built for event scenarios such as RoboCup, integrating technical achievements in reinforcement learning, bionic motion control, and real-time environmental perception.

Unitree G1 Comp Technical Specifications

Robot Height 1320mm
Robot Weight 35kg
Walking Speed 2m/s
Degrees of Freedom 31
Motor Type Torso Robot Integrated Joint (Max Torque 120 N·m)
Sensors Camera, Intel RealSense D455 (Infrared Disabled); Gyroscope, 6-axis High-Stability IMU
Control Unit High-Performance Real-Time Motion Control Unit
Perception & Decision Unit NVIDIA Jetson Orin 16GB (Edge Computing Platform)
Material Aluminum Alloy & High-Strength Engineering Plastic
Battery 13S Ternary Lithium Battery
